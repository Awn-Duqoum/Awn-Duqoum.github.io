Q. What is the purpose of Engineering Orientation Week?

A. Engineering Orientation Week is an exciting time in which you are introduced to the Faculty of Engineering at the University of Waterloo in a safe and fun environment. You will be placed into one of twelve colour groups in an attempt to not only win the coveted title of the week, but to learn more about the University and make lifelong friendships.

Q. How can I be a part of Engineering Orientation Week?

A. Here's a quick summary of the details: When: Tuesday, September 7, 2010 at 9 AM.
Where: Engineering Buildings
Please follow the signs directly to your headquarters (or just look at a map)
What to Wear: It is important to dress appropriately for the weather. During the day, you will be outside having fun in the sun, so wear something comfortable! We would recommend wearing closed toed shoes, comfortable pants or shorts and sunglasses. You will be given a colour group t-shirt at the beginning of the first day. Since there are so many different events as part of Engineering Orientation Week, we cannot guarantee that you won't get wet or dirty!
What to Bring: Make sure you are always wearing your wrist band.
What Not to Bring: Electronic devices including cell phones, cameras, mp3 players, your wallet, valuables, your back pack, alcohol and drugs.
Special Circumstances: Should you have any special circumstances, such as a food allergy or a physical disability, please feel free to contact us at foc@engmail.uwaterloo.ca and we will do our best to accommodate your needs.

Q. I noticed that one day we have the ELPE scheduled - what is that?

 

A. The ELPE is the English Language Proficiency Exam. This is not something to stress about. It is simply designed to assess your written English skills. Although you are required to either pass the ELPE or to take an English credit, it is not recommended that you study for this exam. The results of this test will not count against any student.

Another exam you will be taking is the Math Preparedness Test during your first week of school. The location and contents of this exam will be discussed during your first calculus lecture of the term (it tests some basic math skills that you probably already possess if you got this far). Don't study for this one either - the "worst" thing that can happen if you do poorly on this exam is that you'll be invited to attend free tutoring sessions - if you need to study to pass it, you may as well get the extra help!

Q. Are we allowed to bring marijuana to Orientation Week?

 

A. Officially, marijuana has not been decriminalized and UW is still enforcing a zero tolerance policy for both possession and use. Thus our message at the current time is the same as always. UW enforces a zero tolerance policy for drug use and possession.

Please be warned that the University also enforces a strict underage drinking policy: if you're caught drinking underage in any establishment on campus, you will be banned from all establishments on campus and your access will be suspended until one year after your 19th birthday.
